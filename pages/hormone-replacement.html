Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about hormone replacement therapy

How do I know if I need hormone replacement therapy?

Common signs include fatigue, mood changes, low libido, weight gain, sleep issues, and decreased mental clarity. A comprehensive consultation and lab testing can determine if HRT is right for you.

How long does it take to see results?

Many patients notice improvements within 2-4 weeks, with optimal results typically achieved within 3-6 months as hormone levels stabilize.

Are bioidentical hormones safe?

When properly prescribed and monitored, bioidentical hormones are generally very safe. We use comprehensive monitoring to ensure optimal safety and effectiveness.

What's the difference between bioidentical and synthetic hormones?

Bioidentical hormones have the same molecular structure as hormones naturally produced by your body, while synthetic hormones have different structures and may cause more side effects.

How often will I need follow-up appointments?

Initially, follow-ups are more frequent (every 4-6 weeks), then typically every 3-6 months once levels are optimized, with annual comprehensive evaluations.

Does insurance cover hormone replacement therapy?

Coverage varies by insurance plan and medical necessity. We provide detailed receipts for insurance submission and offer flexible payment options.
